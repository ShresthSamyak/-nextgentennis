# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Parents (and guardians) of kids and teens, roughly ages 4–18, in the greater
Phoenix, Arizona area. They are evaluating youth tennis programs on a phone or
laptop, often after a recommendation or a search. Their job: quickly judge
whether this coaching is credible, age-appropriate, and worth their child's
time — then register without friction. Secondary readers: the teens themselves
(13+ outdoor track), who want it to feel cool, not childish.

## Product Purpose

NextGen Tennis is a coaching brand, not a facility. It runs engaging **indoor
training for ages 4–12** and **outdoor development for ages 13+**, using partner
courts across the Phoenix valley (no owned facility). The site exists to convert
interested parents into registrations and to communicate a clear development
pathway from a first racquet to competitive play. Success = a parent finishes
the registration form feeling confident their kid is in good hands.

## Brand Personality

Energetic, confident, athletic — with warmth. The voice is encouraging and
direct, never corporate or babyish. Think a youth-sports program with the craft
of a real consumer brand: Nike Kids energy, USTA credibility, Apple-level
clarity. Three words: **kinetic, credible, encouraging.**

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-generated landing pages: tiny tracked uppercase eyebrows above
  every section, identical icon+heading+text card grids, colored-block
  placeholders where photos belong, gradient text, side-stripe accent borders.
- Stiff/corporate sports-club sites (clip-art trophies, stocky template feel).
- Babyish primary-color "kids zone" aesthetics that teens would be embarrassed by.
- Editorial-magazine cosplay (display-serif + italic + drop caps) — wrong lane
  for an athletic brand.

## Design Principles

- **Show the energy.** Real action photography of kids playing carries the
  brand; never a colored rectangle where a photo belongs.
- **One clear next step.** Every screen drives toward "register your child";
  reduce everything that competes with that.
- **Age-legible, not age-segregated.** Indoor 4–12 vs outdoor 13+ should always
  be obvious at a glance, without making the teen track feel like daycare.
- **Earned motion.** Motion is choreographed where it adds energy (hero, key
  reveals), not a uniform fade on every block. Reduced-motion always honored.
- **Craft over template.** Vary rhythm and composition; if a section could be
  guessed from the category alone, push further.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (watch lime-on-white and
white-on-lime — lime is a light color, so it needs dark text/ink on it, never
white). Full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, honest alt text on every
photo, and a complete `prefers-reduced-motion` path for all animation.
